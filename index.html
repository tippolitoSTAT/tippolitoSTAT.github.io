Thomas E. Ippolito is a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in statistics at Northwestern University. His research interest concerns the development and analysis of statistical methods used to measure the economic effects of shareholder activism. Mr. Ippolito is co-advised by Larry Hedges, Board of Trustees Professor of Statistics and Elizabeth Tipton, Associate Professor of Statistics. He is a graduate student member of the Statistics for Evidence-Based Policy and Practice Center (STEPP) at Northwestern University and a member of the American Statistical Society.

Prior to doctoral studies at Northwestern University, Mr. Ippolito worked at the Wharton School Department of Statistics and Data Science under the supervision of Paul Shaman, Professor Emeritus of Statistics. Mr. Ippolito is a veteran of the United States Marine Corps and is a graduate of Northwestern University, University of Pennsylvania, and University of Miami.

Interest:

  • Causal Inference for Time Series Analysis
  • Event Study Methodology
  • Policy and Decision Making
  • Social Statistics
  • Shareholder Activism
